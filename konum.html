Geolocation

Denizli is located in the southwest of the Anatolian Peninsula and in the east of the Aegean Region. It is a gateway between Aegean, Central Anatolia and Mediterranean Regions. Denizli province has lands on both regions. Denizli province is located between 28° 30' – 29° 30' east meridians and 37° 12' – 38° 12' north parallels. It is bordered by Burdur and Afyon from the east, Aydın and Manisa from the west, Uşak from the north and Muğla from the south.

The area of the province is 12,134 km², the province constitutes approximately 1.5% of Turkey and 18.5% of the Aegean Region. The height of the center from the sea is 354 meters. The closest place to the sea surface is Sarayköy district with an altitude of 170 meters, and the farthest point is Çameli district with an altitude of 1350 meters.
